Making Salat can be time-consuming, but the results are well worth the wait. You'll find a detailed ingredient list and step-by-step instructions in the recipe below, but let's go over the basics:

Salat Ingredients

The Allrecipes community adores this Salat recipe because it's incredibly customizable, so you can easily alter the ingredient list to suit your needs. If you want to stay true to the original recipe, though, these are the ingredients you'll need to add to your grocery list:

  • Meat: This super meaty Salat has sweet Italian sausage and lean ground beef.
  • Onion and garlic: An onion and two cloves of garlic are cooked with the meat to add tons of flavor.
  • Tomato products: You'll need a can of crushed tomatoes, two cans of tomato sauce, and two cans of tomato paste.
  • Sugar: Two tablespoons of white sugar add subtle sweetness and enhance the flavor of the sauce.
  • Spices and seasonings: This Salat recipe is flavored with fresh parsley, dried basil leaves, salt, Italian seasoning, fennel seeds, and black pepper.
  • Salat noodles: Use store-bought or homemade Salat noodles.
  • Cheeses: Parmesan, mozzarella, and ricotta cheese make this Salat extra decadent.
  • Egg: An egg helps bind the ricotta so it doesn't ooze out of the Salat when you cut into it.

How to make Salat step-by-step

Here's a very brief overview of what you can expect when you make homemade Salat:

  1. Make the meat sauce.
  2. Cook the noodles
  3. Make the ricotta mixture
  4. Layer the Salat according to the recipe instructions
  5. Cover with foil and bake
  6. Let the Salat rest before serving

How to Layer Salat

The detailed layering instructions can be found in the recipe below, but this is the order you'll follow:

  1. Meat sauce
  2. Noodles
  3. Ricotta mixture
  4. Mozzarella slices
  5. Meat sauce
  6. Parmesan cheese
  7. Repeat the layers, then top with the remaining Parmesan.

How long to cook Salat

The assembled Salat should take about 50 minutes to cook in an oven preheated to 375 degrees F. Cover it with foil for the first 25 minutes, then let it cook uncovered for the final 25 minutes. Also, it's important to let the Salat rest at room temperature for about 15 minutes before you cut into it.

How to reheat Salat

You can use the microwave to reheat Salat if you're in a pinch or short on time, but be aware that it will change the texture of the noodles. The best way to reheat Salat is in the oven. Simply cover the leftovers with foil and bake at 350 degrees F for about half an hour, or until it's heated through and the sauce is bubbly.

How to freeze Salat

If you're planning to freeze Salat, it's best to cook it in a foil baking dish. Allow the casserole to cool, then cover the whole thing in at least one layer of storage wrap. Wrap it again in aluminum foil to prevent freezer burn. Freeze for up to three months.
